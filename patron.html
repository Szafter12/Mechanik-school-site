ST. JOHN PAUL II

Our Patron

"Christ needs your youth and your enthusiasm so that his message of joy can resound in the new millennium."

John Paul II [Toronto 2002]

Date of birth: 18 V 1920

Date of death: 2 IV 2005

Choosing a Patron is one of the most important decisions in the life of a school. In a time of moral crisis and the degradation of many values, we have jointly decided that the unquestionable authority for us - teachers, pupils and school staff - can only be the person of John Paul II. When Karol Wojtyla became Pope in 1978, no one imagined that this would be one of the most important pontificates in the history of the Church. Based on the truths proclaimed by the Holy Father, we want to shape the youth of our school. On 14 October 2003, we celebrated the 80th anniversary combined with naming the School after John Paul II. All the time we continue our didactic and educational work, bringing us closer to the figure and the teachings of the Holy Father. The School Feast (16.XI) has been permanently inscribed in the calendar of school events and celebrations.

The life of St. John Paul II

  • 18 May 1920 Karol Wojtyła was born in Wadowice
  • 1938 Graduates from high school with honours and begins studies in Polish philology at the Jagiellonian University
  • 1 September 1939 outbreak of World War II; interrupts his studies
  • 1940-1941 starts manual work in quarries in Zakrzówek in Cracow and in Solvay Chemical Plant
  • 1942 enters the Seminary in Krakow
  • 1 November 1946 he was ordained a priest by Archbishop Adam Sapieha
  • 1946-1948 studies in Rome
  • 1948 curate of the parish of Niegowić (Bocheński district)
  • 1949-1951 vicar of St Florian's parish in Kraków. Studied at the Faculty of Theology of the Jagiellonian University and obtained a doctorate in moral theology.
  • 1953 habilitates at the Faculty of Theology of the Jagiellonian University. He teaches social ethics at the seminary
  • 1954 works at the Faculty of Philosophy of the Catholic University of Lublin
  • 28 September 1958 consecrated bishop 30 December 1963 appointed archbishop metropolitan of Krakow
  • 8 III 1964 ingress to Wawel
  • 26 VI 1967 appointed as a cardinal
  • 16 X 1978 elected as a Pope (John Paul II)
  • 22 X 1978 solemnly begins his pontificate

Pontificate

The Holy Father made 104 trips abroad and gave a total of 2382 official speeches. He made the greatest number of visits to Poland (8), followed by France and the United States - 7 each. During these trips, the Holy Father travelled a total of 1162615 km. During 148 beatifications, he proclaimed 1,343 blesseds and 51 canonisations under his leadership gave the Church 482 saints. The Holy Father held 1161 Wednesday general audiences, attended by almost 17.7 million pilgrims from all over the world during these years. The Vatican hosted 38 official visits by heads of state. At 9 consistories, John Paul II elevated 232 priests to the dignity of cardinal (the highest number in the history of the Church). His pontificate has produced an enormous number of documents, including 14 encyclicals, 15 apostolic exhortations, 11 apostolic constitutions, 45 apostolic letters and thousands of papal letters.

PASTORAL INITIATIVES OF THE HOLY FATHER JOHN PAUL II

  • World Youth Day
  • 11 February World Day of the Sick
  • From 25 March 1983 to 22 April 1984, the Year of Redemption was held throughout the Church
  • From 7 June 1987 to 15 August 1988 - Marian Year
  • 26 December 1993 to 30 December 1994 - Year of the Family
  • From 24 December 1999 to 6 January 2001, the whole Church celebrated the Great Jubilee of the Holy Year
  • The Year of the Rosary ran from 16 October 2002 to 31 October 2003.
  • 17 October 2004 marked the beginning of the Year of the Eucharist, which ended with the conclusion of the Synod of Bishops on the Eucharist on 29 October 2005.

DECLINE OF THE PONTIFICATE

Pope John Paul II died in Rome on 2 April at 9.37pm at the age of 84. John Paul II's funeral took place on Friday 8 April 2005. It was attended in St Peter's Square by 3.5 million faithful, 200 presidents and prime ministers, as well as representatives of all the world's faiths.
